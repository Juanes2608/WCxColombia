1. Interim injunction. The governing guidance is American Cyanamid Co v Ethicon
Ltd [1975] AC 396: is there a serious question to be tried, and where does the
balance of convenience lie. (NOTE: the corpus holds this case under the neutral
citation [1975] UKHL 1 — it must still match by name.)

2. Duty of care. See Caparo Industries v Dickman (cited by name only, with no
neutral citation) for the three-stage test.

3. Negligent misstatement. See Hedley Burne & Co v Heller [1964] AC 465 (NOTE:
the party name is deliberately misspelt "Burne" for "Byrne" to test tolerance).

4. Foundational principle. Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562; also cited in its
parallel form M'Alister (or Donoghue) v Stevenson — the same decision.
