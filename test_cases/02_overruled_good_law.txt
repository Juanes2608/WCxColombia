ADVICE ON LIABILITY — Defective premises and pure economic loss
TEST DOCUMENT 02 (good-law / subsequent-treatment trap)

1. The issue is whether the developer owes a duty in negligence for the cost of
repairing a defective foundation, that loss being purely economic.

2. We submit the developer is liable. A builder and a local authority owe a duty
of care in respect of defective building work under Anns v Merton London Borough
Council [1978] AC 728, applying a two-stage test of proximity followed by policy.
That duty was first imposed on a builder in Dutton v Bognor Regis Urban District
Council [1972] 1 QB 373.

3. On that authority the recoverable loss includes the cost of averting a danger
to the health and safety of the occupiers created by the defect.

4. We acknowledge that the defendant will rely on Murphy v Brentwood District
Council [1991] 1 AC 398, and on the treatment of pure economic loss in Caparo
Industries plc v Dickman [1990] 2 AC 605, but we submit the Anns approach is to
be preferred and remains good law.
