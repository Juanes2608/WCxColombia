CLAIMANT'S SKELETON ARGUMENT ON QUANTUM
TEST DOCUMENT 03 (right case, wrong proposition — "go to source")

1. Expectation profits. The claimant seeks the profits it would have earned had
the contract been performed. The claimant relies on Anglia Television Ltd v Reed
[1972] 1 QB 60 as authority that a claimant may recover its full expectation
profits, including the future profits lost on the abandoned production.

2. Damages at large. The claimant further seeks an enhanced award reflecting the
defendant's conduct, relying on Rookes v Barnard [1964] AC 1129 as the general
authority for compensatory damages at large in contract.

3. Remoteness. The recoverable heads are in any event limited by the contemplation
test in Hadley v Baxendale (1854) 9 Ex 341.
