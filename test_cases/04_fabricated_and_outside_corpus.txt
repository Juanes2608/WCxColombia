RESPONDENT'S SKELETON ARGUMENT
TEST DOCUMENT 04 (fabricated vs. real-but-uncovered)

1. The economic torts. The unlawful means and inducement claims are governed by
the restatement in OBG Ltd v Allan [2007] UKHL 21, building on the principle in
Lumley v Gye (1853) 2 E&B 216.

2. Recent authority. That principle was applied to a procurement group in
Hollingsworth Marine Ltd v Castibel Logistics SA [2020] EWHC 2217 (Comm), where
the court held that a parent company is fixed with the intention to procure a
breach even absent knowledge of the contract's precise terms.

3. Restitutionary measure. Damages assessed by reference to a hypothetical
release fee are available following Wrotham Park Estate Co Ltd v Parkside Homes
Ltd [1974] 1 WLR 798.

4. Consequential loss. On assessment the court should also follow Verbruck
Pharmaceuticals Ltd v NHS Commissioning Board [2019] EWCA Civ 1043 and Trenton
Aviation Holdings Inc v Meridian Underwriting Ltd [2022] EWHC 559 (Comm).
